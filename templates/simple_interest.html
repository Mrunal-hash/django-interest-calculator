What is Simple Interest?

Simple interest is the interest paid on a loan or earned on a savings account that doesn't take compounding into account. It's calculated by multiplying the principal amount by the interest rate and the time period. Below is its mathematical formula:

A = P(1+rt)

  • P = principal amount
  • r = annual interest rate
  • t = time (in years)
  • A = total amount

Interest = A - P
